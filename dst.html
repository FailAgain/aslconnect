Continue your Deaf Studies journey online

ASL Connect offers a variety of Deaf Studies courses for credit. These ASL/English bilingual courses have been designed by leading scholars in the field of Deaf Studies. See below for a description of our Deaf Studies offerings.

Register now

PST 210

Introduction to Deaf Studies (3)

This course introduces students to fundamental aspects of Deaf Studies, revealing unique perspectives on language, culture and identity.

PST 213

Introduction to Cultural Studies (3)

This course introduces students to key concepts in critical theory-- ideology, culture, identity--through a variety of lenses, including Marxism, feminism, critical race theory, disability studies and Deaf Studies.

PST 212

Deaf Culture (3)

This course will begin with a macroscopic view of culture, and then will focus on the microscopic view of the Deaf experience. Students will study important persons, historical events and diversity within the global Deaf community through interdisciplinary approaches.

PST 202

Dynamics of Oppression (3)

This course examines various forms of oppression by looking across different cultures and communities, then examines possible parallels occurring within the deaf community.

PST 203

Oral Traditions in the Deaf Community (3)

Students study ASL literary traditions through ASL poetry, storytelling, and performance.

PST 360

Disability Studies (3)

This course explores the many social and political influences that construct the category of "disability,” while also examining how persons with disabilities construct their own meanings and identities.

PST 222

Deaf Women's Studies (3)

This course explores the intersections of women's studies, feminism and Deaf Studies. Students will explore issues faced by both hearing and deaf women, including career, educational opportunities, reproduction, and patriarchy, among others.

PST 223

Black Deaf People Studies

This course focuses on the social, political, and cultural development of the black deaf community and intersections between the general deaf community and black community.

PST 229

Multicultural Deaf Lives (Topic to be specified) (3)

This course will focus on cultural issues, values, behaviors, identities and language of Deaf people from diverse ethnic and racial backgrounds. Students will examine autobiographies, documentaries, films, videos, and academic literature to help understand the contributions and historical development of the emerging majority of the Deaf community.
